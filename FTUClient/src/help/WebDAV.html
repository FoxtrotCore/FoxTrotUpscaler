WebDAV Upload

Overview

Go ahead, show off these upscaled videos to the world! That means though you need to get them of your computer and on to the Internet. But then using a queue could force you to wait a couple days before you interrupt your computer from upscaling. This also assumes you catch FoxTrotUpscaler just as Stage D finishes, which could be during your sleep. For this reason, FTU has the ability to upload to a WebDAV server, immediately after Stage D completes. The upscaling will take a break for a bit during the upload, but if during a queue, will continue right on with the next episode after. This section will go over this feature, and how to set it up.

Setup

  1. Obtain the necessary credentials for the WebDAV server from its master.
  2. Open FTU, and go to the Preferences tab
  3. Press "Enable Uploads" in the WebDAV section
  4. In the pop-up, enter in the the network address of the folder, enter in your credentials, and press "Verify".
  5. If  verification is successful, the client will place a text file on the folder accessed to prove its connection, and where it plans to upload, and the values will be updated.

Usage

You wont notice anything different with this enabled until a upscale reaches Stage D completion. A dialog box will appear letting the user know it is uploading, with the time elapsed. When completed, the dialog will close, and the next upscale will begin if in a queue. The Overwrite safety check also applies here, and will prevent overwriting on the server. It will however, keep the local copy, since the overwrite check on Process start, prevented overwriting a local version.
A failed upload will throw an error, but continue the next video if there is a queue.Credentials for WebDAV server are saved, even on restarting FTU.
